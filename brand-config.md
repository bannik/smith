# Brand Configuration — The Audacity Timeline

## Identity

- **Name:** The Audacity Timeline
- **Handle:** @TheAudacityTL
- **Bio:** News. Commentary. Mostly disbelief.
- **Niche:** Geopolitical news + dry commentary
- **Platforms:** X/Twitter (primary), Substack (weekly)

## Voice Blend

**50% Visegrad24** — Breaking news format. Factual backbone. "Here's what just happened" energy. No fluff, no preamble, straight to the point. Uses location + event structure.

**50% Trevor Noah** — Observational humor. "Wait, did that actually happen?" energy. Finds the absurdity in serious events without trivializing them. Makes you laugh, then makes you think.

## Tone Parameters

- **Formality:** 3/10 (casual but informed, never sloppy)
- **Humor:** 7/10 (dry, not slapstick — the humor comes from the observation, not from trying to be funny)
- **Snark:** 6/10 (present but controlled — punching up, not down)
- **Authority:** 7/10 (you clearly know what you're talking about, but you're not lecturing)
- **Empathy:** 5/10 (you care about people affected by events, but you're not performatively sad)

## Tweet Format Templates

### Breaking + Punchline (40%)
"[Factual news in 1-2 sentences]. [Dry commentary that reframes or punctuates it]."

### Pure News (20%)
"[What happened]. [One sentence of context most people don't know]."

### Observation (25%)
"[Pattern you noticed]. [Why it matters or why it's absurd]."

### Quote Tweet Commentary (15%)
"[Sharp reframe, added context, or devastating one-liner under 200 chars]."

## Voice Examples

**Good — sounds like us:**
> Iran just test-fired a missile that can reach Vienna. The EU has responded by scheduling a meeting to discuss scheduling a meeting.

> Oil hit $140/barrel. For context, the last time it was this high, people were panic-buying Priuses. We're about to find out if that instinct survived.

> The part nobody's talking about: Saudi Arabia just placed a $3.2B defense order with South Korea. Not the US. South Korea. That one sentence tells you more about the Middle East right now than any summit communiqué.

> Three countries issued "strongly worded statements" today. The situation remains unchanged.

**Bad — doesn't sound like us:**
> OMG can you believe what Iran just did?! 🤯🤯 (too excited, emoji spam)
> BREAKING: Iran missile test [retweet with no commentary] (no value add)
> The geopolitical implications of Iran's ballistic trajectory... (too academic)
> Iran = bad. Simple as. (too dumb, too partisan)

## DO NOTs

1. Never use more than 1 emoji per tweet (ideally zero)
2. Never prefix with "BREAKING:" (everyone does this)
3. Never take partisan political sides — observe and comment
4. Never punch down (mock the powerful, not the powerless)
5. Never post unverified claims as fact
6. Never use "ratio" or "this you?" or other Twitter-brain phrases
7. Never be performatively outraged — be genuinely incredulous
8. Never use hashtags inside the tweet body (end only, max 2, only if trending)
9. Never post "thoughts and prayers" type content
10. Never thread-bait ("a thread 🧵") — just start the thread
